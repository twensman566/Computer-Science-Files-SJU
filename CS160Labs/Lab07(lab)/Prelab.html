Pre-lab exercise: A sliding box

Start DrJava and open the file BoxController.java from your lab directory. The BoxController class is similar to others that we have seen that extend the GraphicsProgram class. Its init() method simply creates one line of text (a GLabel), and its mouseClicked() method hides that text and creates an ActiveBox object at the point of the click. The ActiveBox will take care of itself, moving across the screen from left to right.

Now open the file ActiveBox.java; in it you will see a template for the ActiveBox class. It defines constants for the time to pause between movements (DELAY_TIME) and the speed at which to move across the canvas (X_SPEED). You are to complete the code for the run() method.

The run() method will consist of a while-loop that runs as long as the box has not moved beyond the right edge of the canvas. You'll need to find a boolean expression involving the box size, the current x-position of the box and the width of the canvas to control the loop; use the getX() and getWidth() methods, respectively, to get those two values. Inside the loop, the box moves to the right by a distance of X_SPEED, and then pauses for the defined delay time. After the while-loop, remove the box from the canvas using the remove() method.

Show your work to the TA or lab instructor at the beginning of lab.
