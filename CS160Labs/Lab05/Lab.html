Lab 5: Loops and classes—bullseyes and bowling pins

In this lab, you will gain experience defining classes and implementing methods that require the use of loops.

Preliminaries

Complete the pre-lab exercise, if you have not already done so, before going on to the exercises below.

Part 1: basic bowling pins

Like the bullseye program, the bowling pin program is in two files. The first, BowlingPinPlacer.java, is provided for you. It is a simple program that creates a new BowlingPins object. Your job is to complete the second file, BowlingPins.java, to define the BowlingPins class that places 10 circles representing ten bowling pins lined up in 4 rows, of 4, 3, 2 and 1 pins, respectively. Your final configuration of circles should be configured something like the diagram below. The back left pin should be located at the x- and y-coordinates defined in BowlingPinPlacer.java. You will know if your program is correct, if the red pin created in BowlingPinPlacer.java completely overlaps the back left pin of your configuration. The size of the pins (diameter), the horizontal spacing between pins and vertical spacing between rows should be declared as static final constants. (Note that the horizontal spacing should be about 2 ⁄√3 times the vertical distance if you want equilateral triangles; you should experiment with the constants to get pleasing values.)
